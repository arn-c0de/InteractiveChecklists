# Military Aviation Radio Communication Checklist (English, US/NATO style)

## 0. Initial Contact / Check-in

- **Pilot (single aircraft)**:
  - [ ] "[Station], [Callsign], check-in, [position], [altitude], [intentions]."

- **Pilot (formation)**:
  - [ ] "[Station], [Callsign] flight, check-in, [formation position], [position], [altitude], [intentions]."

- **Examples / Notes:**
  - State your callsign clearly (e.g., "Raven 21").
  - Report position relative to a fix/field or a bearing/distance (e.g., "5 miles west of CHARLIE VOR").
  - Use altitudes in feet MSL or flight levels as appropriate (e.g., "1500 feet" or "FL180").

---

## 1. Clearance / Startup

- **Pilot:**
  - [ ] "Clearance Delivery, [Station], [Callsign], request IFR clearance to [destination]."

- **ATC (Clearance Delivery):**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], cleared to [destination] via [route], climb to [altitude], departure frequency [freq], squawk [code]."

- **Pilot (Readback)**:
  - [ ] "Cleared to [destination] via [route], climb to [altitude], departure frequency [freq], squawk [code], [Callsign]."

- **Pilot (Startup)**:
  - [ ] "Ground, [Callsign], request start-up."  
  - If hot-pit or special start, include special instruction (e.g., "hot-pit start, [Callsign]").

---

## 2. Taxi / Ground Operations

- **Pilot:**
  - [ ] "Ground, [Callsign], request taxi to [parking/runway/point] via [taxiways]."

- **ATC:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], taxi to runway [XX] via [taxiways], hold short of [XX]."

- **Pilot (Readback):**
  - [ ] "Taxi to runway [XX] via [taxiways], hold short of [XX], [Callsign]."

- **Crossing / Runup / Special:**
  - To cross another runway: "Request crossing of runway [XX]".
  - On run-up complete: "[Callsign], ready for departure." (don't assume this gives clearance).

---

## 3. Line-Up / Takeoff

- **Pilot (Holding Short)**:
  - [ ] "Tower, [Callsign], holding short runway [XX], ready."  

- **ATC (Line-Up):**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], line up and wait runway [XX]."  
  - Or: "[Callsign], hold short of runway [XX]."

- **Pilot (Readback)**:
  - [ ] "Line up and wait runway [XX], [Callsign]."

- **ATC (Clearance):**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], runway [XX], cleared for takeoff."  

- **Pilot (Readback)**:
  - [ ] "Cleared for takeoff runway [XX], [Callsign]."

- **Aborted Takeoff / Reject:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign] aborting takeoff" or "rejecting takeoff" — follow with intentions and taxi instructions.

---

## 4. Departure / Climb

- **Pilot (Initial Contact)**:
  - [ ] "Departure, [Callsign], airborne runway [XX], passing [altitude], climbing to [altitude]."

- **ATC:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], radar contact. Climb and maintain [altitude]" or "[Callsign], advise when passing [altitude]."

---

## 5. Enroute / Control

- **Pilot (Climb / Level Change)**:
  - [ ] "Center/Control, [Callsign], request climb to [altitude]."

- **Pilot (Block Request)**:
  - [ ] "Center, [Callsign], request block [altitude band]."

- **MARSA / Formation:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign] requests MARSA" — after confirmation, both pilots and ATC are aware that the flight adheres to MARSA procedures.

- **Flight Formations:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign] flight of [number]" — use formation suffixes (e.g., "Raven 1 flight of 2").

---

## 6. Tactical / Military-Specific

- **Fence In/Fence Out:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign] fence in" — call when taking off / progressing into mission area; other participants acknowledge.
  - [ ] "[Callsign] fence out" — call when leaving the mission area or returning to base.

- **Ops / Systems Check:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], ops check [item]" or "ops check complete".

- **Abort / Knock It Off:**
  - [ ] "Knock it off" — universal tactical command to immediately cease hazardous maneuvering.  
  - [ ] "Terminate" or "Abort" as required by specific mission protocols.

- **Lost Comm / Radio Check:**
  - If you experience radio failure: squawk 7600, attempt VHF guard (121.5 MHz), and follow the NORDO recovery plan.  
  - To check radio from flight: "[Callsign] radio check" or "[Callsign] monitor [freq]".

---

## 7. Descent / Approach

- **Pilot:**
  - [ ] "Approach, [Callsign], request descent, [position/intentions]."  

- **Pilot (Recovery)**:
  - [ ] "Approach, [Callsign], request recovery to [field], [intentions]."  

- **ATC:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], descend and maintain [altitude]" or "[Callsign], join final approach course, cleared to [approach type]."

---

## 8. Initial / Overhead Pattern (Military)

- **Pilot:**
  - [ ] "Tower, [Callsign], initial [runway]."

- **Tower:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], break approved" or "[Callsign], break left/right approved" (with direction).

- **Pilot (Readback):**
  - [ ] "Break approved, [Callsign]."

- **Pilot (Configuration Calls)**:
  - [ ] "[Callsign], gear down" / "[Callsign], flaps set" / "[Callsign], gear up."  

---

## 9. Final / Landing

- **Pilot (Final)**:
  - [ ] "[Callsign], final runway [XX]."

- **ATC (Clearance to Land):**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], cleared to land runway [XX]."

- **Pilot (Readback):**
  - [ ] "Cleared to land runway [XX], [Callsign]."

- **Waveoff / Missed Approach:**
  - [ ] "Going around" or "Missed approach, [Callsign]" and follow published missed approach or ATC instructions.

---

## 10. After Landing

- **Pilot:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], clear of runway [XX]."

- **ATC:**
  - [ ] "[Callsign], taxi to parking [or as instructed]."

---

## 11. Emergency / Abnormal

- **Mayday (Distress):**
  - [ ] "Mayday, Mayday, Mayday, [Callsign], [location], [nature of emergency], [assistance required], [number of souls on board], [fuel remaining], [intentions]."

- **Pan-Pan (Urgency):**
  - [ ] "Pan-Pan, Pan-Pan, Pan-Pan, [Callsign], [nature of urgency]."

- **Fuel States:**
  - [ ] "Minimum fuel" (civil) or "Bingo fuel" (military/mission planning) — use conservative values and follow ATO/Flight plan guidance.

- **NORDO / Radio Failure:**
  - [ ] "If NORDO, squawk 7600; if emergency, squawk 7700. Attempt guard frequency 121.5 MHz and follow missed-comm or Lost Com recovery plan." 

---

## 12. Standard Terms

- **Roger** – I have received and understood your last transmission.
- **Wilco** – Will comply with the instructions.
- **Affirmative / Negative** – Yes / No.
- **Unable** – Cannot comply.
- **Standby** – Wait; suspended action until further instruction.
- **Say again** – Repeat message.
- **Check** – Verify.

- **Additional Common Terms and Actions:**
  - **Squawk [code]** – Set transponder to assigned code (e.g., "Squawk 0123").
  - **Squawk 7600** – Radio failure (NORDO); **Squawk 7700** – General emergency for all aircraft.
  - **Report [altitude/position]** – Provide requested information.
  - **Contact [agency/freq]** – Switch to ATC agency or frequency indicated.
  - **Say again** / **Confirm** – Use to request or confirm critical information.
