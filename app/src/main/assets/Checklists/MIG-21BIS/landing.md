# MiG-21bis Landing Checklist

## 1. Approach and Configuration

- [ ] **Speed Reduction:** Reduce speed to approximately 500 km/h at around 1000m AGL. Use airbrakes if necessary.
- [ ] **Landing Gear:** At 500 km/h and 1000m AGL, extend the landing gear. Wait for three green lights, then return the gear lever to the middle position.
- [ ] **Flaps (Takeoff):** Below 500 km/h and at 600m AGL, set flaps to the takeoff position. Continue to slow to around 400 km/h.
- [ ] **Flaps (Landing):** At 300m AGL and 380 km/h, set flaps to the landing position.
- [ ] **Autopilot/Stabilizer:** Turn off the autopilot stabilizer if it is on.
- [ ] **Fuel:** Ensure fuel weight is not excessive for landing.
- [ ] **Landing Light:** Turn on the landing light if required.

## 2. Final Approach

- [ ] **Approach Speed:** Maintain an approach speed between 280-330 km/h.
- [ ] **Sink Rate:** Aim for a shallow approach with a descent rate of approximately 5-10 m/s, reducing to 0.5 m/s just before touchdown.
- [ ] **Throttle:** Maintain power during the final approach (83-87% N1 RPM).

## 3. Touchdown

- [ ] **Nose Attitude:** Fly the aircraft onto the ground with a minimal flare, touching down on the main landing gear first.
- [ ] **Touchdown Speed:** Touchdown should occur at 280-240 km/h.
- [ ] **Throttle:** Once the main wheels touch the ground, move the throttle to IDLE.

## 4. Rollout

- [ ] **Nose Wheel:** Use back stick to keep the nose wheel off the ground until speed drops to 240-210 km/h.
- [ ] **Drag Chute:** Deploy the drag chute.
- [ ] **Braking:** Apply wheel brakes by tapping the brake lever.
- [ ] **Chute Jettison:** At taxi speed (around 15-20 km/h), jettison the chute.
