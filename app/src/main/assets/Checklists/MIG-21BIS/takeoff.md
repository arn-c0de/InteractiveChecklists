# MiG-21bis Takeoff Checklist

## 1. Pre-Takeoff Configuration

- [ ] **Canopy:** Ensure the canopy is sealed and secure.
- [ ] **Nosewheel Brake:** Set the nosewheel brake lever to the ON (horizontal) position.
- [ ] **Flaps:** Set flaps to the TAKEOFF position (25 degrees).
- [ ] **SAU Autopilot:** Verify that no lights are lit on the SAU autopilot control panel.
- [ ] **Landing Gear Lever:** Confirm the landing gear lever fixator is unlocked (up).
- [ ] **Trim:** Check that the trim neutral light is illuminated.

## 2. Engine Power and Acceleration

- [ ] **Brakes:** Hold the brakes.
- [ ] **Throttle:** Advance the throttle to maximum power (100%).
- [ ] **Engine Temp:** Wait for the engine exhaust temperature to reach 600 degrees Celsius.
- [ ] **Afterburner:** Engage the afterburner and verify that both the "Nozzle open" and "1st Afterburner" lights are engaged.
- [ ] **Brakes:** Release the wheel brakes.
- [ ] **Steering:** Steer the aircraft using the rudder pedals.

## 3. Rotation and Liftoff

- [ ] **Rotation Speed:** At an indicated airspeed (IAS) of approximately 250-300 km/h, rotate to a 5-10 degree nose-up attitude.
- [ ] **Liftoff Speed:** Liftoff should occur around 350-360 km/h.

## 4. Post-Takeoff Actions and Initial Climb

- [ ] **Landing Gear:** Above 10 meters AGL, retract the landing gear. After moving lever to UP, wait for gear lights to turn red, then place lever in NEUTRAL.
- [ ] **Flaps:** At 100 meters AGL, press the UP flap position button to retract the flaps.
- [ ] **Climb:** Maintain an initial climb at approximately 360-380 km/h with a 15-degree attitude.
- [ ] **Speed & Altitude:** Keep a minimum altitude of 600 meters AGL and a minimum speed of 600 km/h.
- [ ] **Throttle:** Throttle back to around 95% N1 RPM to disengage the afterburner.
