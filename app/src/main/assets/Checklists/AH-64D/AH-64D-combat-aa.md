# AH-64D Apache Air-to-Air Combat Checklist

## Pre-Combat Preparation

- [ ] **Mission brief** - Review air threat
  - Enemy helicopter types (Mi-24, Mi-28, Ka-52, etc.)
  - Fixed-wing threats (A-10, Su-25, etc.)
  - UAV threats
  - Enemy tactics and capabilities
  - Friendly air assets and deconfliction
- [ ] **Crew coordination** - Confirm roles
  - CPG: Target acquisition, weapons, radar
  - Pilot: Aircraft maneuvering, situational awareness
- [ ] **Air-to-air weapons check (WPN page)** - Verify
  - **Stinger missiles (ATAS)** - Quantity (typically 2-4)
  - **30mm gun** - Rounds available (for backup engagement)
  - **Hellfire** - Can be used against slow/hovering helicopters
- [ ] **Fuel state** - Verify sufficient for engagement and egress
- [ ] **Escape routes** - Plan
  - Terrain to mask behind if engaged
  - Friendly air defense coverage areas

## Air-to-Air Systems Setup

- [ ] **Master arm switch** - ARM (when entering threat area)
- [ ] **FCR mode** - Set for air search
  - **AIR TGT** mode - Air target search and track
  - Press AIR TGT on FCR panel or WPN page
- [ ] **ASE (Aircraft Survivability Equipment)** - All active
  - RWR - ON (detect enemy radar)
  - CMWS - ON (missile warning)
  - LWS - ON (laser warning)
  - Chaff/Flare programs loaded
- [ ] **Stinger selected** - If air threat imminent
  - Press ATAS button on WPN page
  - Stinger becomes active weapon
  - Missile status displayed
- [ ] **TADS** - Active for visual acquisition
  - DTV for day operations
  - FLIR for night/low visibility
  - Can supplement FCR search
- [ ] **IFF (Identification Friend or Foe)** - Interrogate contacts
  - Mode 4 for military IFF
  - Prevents fratricide

## Air Target Search and Detection

### FCR Air Search

- [ ] **FCR AIR TGT mode** - Active
  - FCR scans for airborne targets
  - Detection range: ~8-10 km for helicopters, farther for fast jets
- [ ] **FCR scan pattern** - Automatic 90-degree scan
  - Azimuth: ±45 degrees from aircraft nose
  - Elevation: Adjusts automatically
- [ ] **Target detection** - FCR paints contacts on TSD
  - Air targets displayed as diamond symbols
  - Altitude and range information shown
- [ ] **Target sorting** - Evaluate contacts
  - Closing vs opening (closure rate)
  - High vs low altitude
  - Fast vs slow (helicopter vs jet)
- [ ] **Contact prioritization** - Threat assessment
  - Closest/fastest closing = highest threat
  - FCR auto-prioritizes in some modes

### Visual Search (TADS/PNVS)

- [ ] **Crew visual scan** - Both crew continuously scan
  - Pilot: PNVS or unaided vision
  - CPG: TADS or unaided vision
- [ ] **TADS air search** - CPG scans horizon
  - WIDE FOV for search
  - Look for movement, contrails, glints
  - Day: Look for aircraft silhouette
  - Night: FLIR hot spots (engine exhausts)
- [ ] **Visual contact call** - Immediate crew coordination
  - "Contact, helicopter, 2 o'clock, 3 kilometers, level"
  - Pilot confirms: "Visual" or "Negative contact"

## Target Identification

- [ ] **IFF interrogation** - Query contact
  - Use IFF system to interrogate
  - Friendly response = Mode 4 reply
  - No response = Continue ID process (may be hostile or system off)
- [ ] **Visual ID via TADS** - CPG zooms in
  - Slew TADS to contact
  - NARROW or ZOOM FOV
  - Identify aircraft type
  - Look for hostile markings/configuration
- [ ] **Range to target** - Determine via FCR or TADS laser
  - FCR provides continuous range
  - TADS laser for precision (if within laser range ~8 km)
- [ ] **Aspect and closure** - Assess target geometry
  - Hot (head-on) = closing rapidly
  - Beam (crossing) = flanking
  - Cold (tail-on) = opening/running
- [ ] **Positive hostile ID** - Confirm per ROE
  - Visual confirmation
  - IFF negative
  - In hostile area
  - Hostile actions (firing, maneuvering aggressively)
- [ ] **Engagement authority** - ROE check
  - Are you cleared to engage?
  - Confirm no friendlies in area

## Stinger (ATAS) Missile Employment

### Stinger Selection and Setup

- [ ] **Stinger selected** - Press ATAS on WPN page
  - Stinger symbology on TADS and IHADSS
- [ ] **Stinger inventory** - Check WPN page
  - Typically 2-4 missiles on ATAS pylons
  - Verify missiles READY (no faults)
- [ ] **Seeker status** - Verify missile ready
  - Seeker cooled and operational
  - No red X on missile icon

### Stinger Engagement Parameters

- [ ] **Range to target** - Verify within envelope
  - **Minimum range:** ~500 m (seeker/motor arm distance)
  - **Optimal range:** 1,000-4,000 m
  - **Maximum range:** ~5,000-8,000 m (depending on target aspect and speed)
- [ ] **Target altitude** - Verify engageable
  - Stinger can engage targets up to ~10,000 ft AGL
  - Best performance against low-medium altitude (below 5,000 ft)
- [ ] **Target aspect** - Assess shot geometry
  - **Hot (head-on):** Harder lock (small IR signature), shorter time to impact
  - **Beam (side):** Good lock, medium time
  - **Stern (tail):** Best lock (engine IR signature), longest time
- [ ] **Airspeed (own aircraft)** - Maintain stable
  - Hover to 60 KIAS optimal for steady aim
  - Avoid high-G maneuvers during engagement

### Stinger Launch Sequence

- [ ] **Target acquisition** - TADS or PNVS on target
  - Slew TADS to target or point aircraft at target
  - Keep target in field of view
- [ ] **Stinger uncage** - Prepare seeker
  - Missile seeker activates and searches for IR source
  - Seeker FOV displayed on TADS/IHADSS
- [ ] **Seeker tone** - Listen for lock-on
  - Low growl = Seeker tracking IR source
  - High-pitched tone = STRONG LOCK, ready to fire
  - Tone in headset (both crew can hear)
- [ ] **Lock verification** - Confirm good lock
  - High-pitched seeker tone continuous
  - LOCK indication on WPN page
  - Missile reticle tracking target on TADS
- [ ] **Clear field of fire** - Verify
  - No obstacles between aircraft and target
  - Missile flight path clear
  - Safe distance from rotor arc
- [ ] **Launch authorization** - Final check
  - Positive hostile ID
  - ROE met
  - Lock solid
- [ ] **Missile launch** - Execute
  - Press weapon trigger on TEDAC (CPG) or cyclic (Pilot)
  - Missile motor ignites and launches
  - "MISSILE AWAY" audio callout
  - CPG calls: "Fox Two" (IR missile launch call)
- [ ] **Post-launch maneuver** - Immediate
  - Break/jink after launch (do not fly predictably)
  - Missile is fire-and-forget (no guidance required)
  - Maintain visual on target and missile if able

### Stinger Missile Flight

- [ ] **Missile tracking** - Observe
  - Stinger is IR homing, fully autonomous
  - No pilot input after launch
  - Time of flight: ~5-15 seconds depending on range
- [ ] **Target reaction** - Monitor
  - Does target maneuver (aware of launch)?
  - Does target deploy flares?
  - Is missile tracking or distracted by flares?
- [ ] **Impact** - Assess
  - Visual confirmation of hit
  - Target explosion, fire, or crash
  - Or missile miss (target survives)

### Stinger Re-Attack

- [ ] **First missile assessment** - Did it hit?
  - If hit: Assess damage, target still flying?
  - If miss: Immediate re-attack
- [ ] **Second missile** - Launch if available
  - Reacquire target (may be maneuvering)
  - New lock-on required
  - Repeat launch sequence
- [ ] **Gun backup** - If out of Stingers
  - Transition to 30mm gun for engagement

## 30mm Gun Air-to-Air Employment

### Gun AA Setup

- [ ] **Gun selected** - Press GUN on WPN page
- [ ] **Gun mode** - TADS or Fixed Forward
  - **TADS slaved** - Gun tracks TADS LOS (for crossing shots)
  - **Fixed forward** - Pilot aims (for head-on/stern shots)
- [ ] **Burst limiter** - Set
  - 50 rounds or CONT for air targets (need volume of fire)

### Gun AA Engagement

- [ ] **Range to target** - Determine
  - Optimal: 500-1,000 m
  - Maximum effective: 1,200-1,500 m
  - Closing speed affects time available
- [ ] **Lead computation** - Aim ahead of target
  - Gun computer calculates lead for crossing targets
  - TADS reticle shows computed aim point
  - Manual lead required if fixed forward
- [ ] **Firing solution** - Establish
  - Target in reticle
  - Range acceptable
  - Closure stable
- [ ] **Fire** - Engage
  - Long burst (50-100 rounds)
  - Observe tracers
  - Walk fire onto target if needed (adjust aim)
- [ ] **Ammunition management** - Monitor
  - Air-to-air gun engagements consume ammo rapidly
  - Conserve for multiple engagements if possible

## Defensive Air-to-Air Maneuvers

### Threat Helicopter Engagement (Being Attacked)

- [ ] **Visual acquisition of threat** - Locate attacking helicopter
  - CPG and Pilot both scan
  - Use TADS to zoom on threat
- [ ] **Threat aspect** - Assess
  - Is enemy maneuvering for shot?
  - Is enemy launching weapons?
- [ ] **Defensive actions** - Execute
  - **Break into threat** - Turn toward attacker (beam their shot)
  - **Chaff/Flare** - Deploy if missile launched
  - **Terrain mask** - Dive to terrain, hide behind ridge/trees
  - **Speed** - Max power, gain speed and energy
  - **Jink** - Unpredictable altitude and heading changes
- [ ] **Offensive counter** - Turn fight around
  - If able, reverse to offensive position
  - Maneuver to get on enemy's tail or beam
  - Employ own weapons (Stinger or gun)

### Missile Defensive Maneuvers (IR Missile Inbound)

- [ ] **CMWS warning** - "MISSILE MISSILE MISSILE"
  - Immediate action required
- [ ] **Break turn** - High-G turn toward threat
  - Beam the missile (turn perpendicular to missile flight path)
  - Nose low, max power
- [ ] **Flare deployment** - Continuous
  - Deploy flares (auto-program or manual salvo)
  - Flares decoy IR-seeking missile
- [ ] **Terrain masking** - Dive to ground
  - Get low and behind terrain
  - Missile may lose track or impact ground
- [ ] **Countermaneuver** - Break opposite direction
  - After initial break, reverse turn
  - Missile may overshoot or lose energy

### Fixed-Wing Threat (Jet Aircraft)

- [ ] **Threat identification** - Fast mover detected
  - A-10, Su-25, or fighter jet
  - Much faster than helicopter (200-400+ KIAS)
- [ ] **Defensive priority** - SURVIVE first
  - Engage only if no choice (jet has huge advantage)
  - Primary action: HIDE
- [ ] **Terrain masking** - Immediate
  - Get as low as possible
  - Hide behind hills, trees, buildings
  - Jet has poor low-altitude maneuverability and visibility
- [ ] **Radar signature reduction** - FCR shutdown
  - Turn off FCR (reduces radar detectability)
  - Passive sensors only (TADS, visual)
- [ ] **Flare/Chaff ready** - Defensive systems active
  - If jet fires missile, deploy countermeasures
- [ ] **Stinger engagement** - ONLY if jet slow/close
  - Jet in gun pattern (slow, close, predictable) = possible Stinger shot
  - Lock-on difficult (jet fast and hot)
  - Shot of opportunity only
- [ ] **Communication** - Request help
  - Call for friendly air support (CAP, fighters)
  - Egress area if possible

## Air Combat Maneuvering (ACM)

### Helicopter vs Helicopter

- [ ] **Energy management** - Maintain speed and altitude
  - Speed = maneuverability
  - Altitude = potential energy
  - Don't bleed all speed in turns
- [ ] **Positioning** - Gain advantage
  - **Offensive (6 o'clock):** Behind enemy, ideal Stinger/gun position
  - **Defensive (enemy at 6):** Break, jink, reverse
  - **Neutral (beam):** Maneuver for offensive position
- [ ] **Vertical maneuvering** - Use altitude
  - Climb for energy, dive for speed
  - Pop-up for shot, then dive to mask
- [ ] **Turning fight** - Out-turn enemy
  - Use cyclic aggressively
  - Max power for sustained turn
  - Watch rotor RPM (don't overstress)
- [ ] **Shot opportunity** - Take when available
  - Brief stable position = fire Stinger or gun
  - Don't wait for perfect shot (may not come)

### Breaking Off Engagement

- [ ] **Decision to disengage** - When to bug out
  - Low fuel
  - Low ammo
  - Damage to aircraft
  - Overwhelming enemy force
  - Mission complete
- [ ] **Egress maneuver** - Execute escape
  - Max power, max speed
  - Terrain masking immediately
  - Deploy smoke/flares if pursued
  - Erratic flight path (don't fly straight)
- [ ] **Communication** - Report
  - Wingman: "Breaking off, RTB"
  - Command: "Engaged enemy air, RTB, bingo fuel"

## Post-Engagement Actions

- [ ] **Situational awareness** - Reassess
  - Are there more threats?
  - Where is wingman?
  - Fuel state?
- [ ] **Weapons status** - Check WPN page
  - Stingers remaining
  - Gun rounds remaining
  - Ready for re-engagement if needed
- [ ] **Damage check** - Aircraft status
  - Flight controls normal?
  - Engines normal?
  - Any hits taken?
- [ ] **BDA (Battle Damage Assessment)** - Results
  - Enemy aircraft destroyed/damaged?
  - Confirmed kill or probable?
  - Report to command
- [ ] **Master arm** - SAFE (if clear of threats)
- [ ] **RTB or continue mission** - Decision
  - Fuel and weapons sufficient?
  - Aircraft status OK?
  - Mission requires continuation?

## Crew Coordination for Air-to-Air

- [ ] **Continuous communication** - Both crew talking
  - CPG: "Contact, 11 o'clock, 2 kilometers, level, helicopter"
  - Pilot: "Visual, maneuvering for shot"
  - CPG: "Locked, tone, firing"
  - Pilot: "Missile away, breaking right"
- [ ] **Workload division** - Clear roles
  - CPG: Target acquisition, weapons employment, FCR/TADS
  - Pilot: Aircraft maneuvering, navigation, SA
- [ ] **Mutual support** - Both crew scan for threats
  - CPG may be head-down in TADS = Pilot watches for other threats
  - Pilot maneuvering = CPG maintains target track
- [ ] **Brevity codes** - Use standard calls
  - "Tally" - Visual on target
  - "Fox Two" - IR missile launch (Stinger)
  - "Guns guns guns" - Gun firing
  - "Defensive" - Being engaged, maneuvering defensively
  - "Winchester" - Out of ammo
  - "Bingo" - Low fuel, must RTB
