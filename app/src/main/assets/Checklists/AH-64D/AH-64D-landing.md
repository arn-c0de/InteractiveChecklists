# AH-64D Apache Landing Checklist

## Pre-Landing Preparation (10-15 nm from destination)

- [ ] **ATIS (Automatic Terminal Information Service)** - Monitor
  - VHF/UHF frequency for destination
  - Note active runway, winds, altimeter setting, weather
- [ ] **Altimeter setting** - Set on FLT page
  - Press BARO button on MPD
  - Set QNH from ATIS
- [ ] **Landing clearance** - Request from tower/approach
  - "Tower, Apache 123, 10 miles north, inbound for landing"
  - Receive: Runway, pattern entry, altimeter, taxi instructions
- [ ] **Landing area identification** - Locate and verify
  - Runway/helipad/FARP
  - Check TSD for position
  - Visual acquisition of field
- [ ] **Wind check** - Note from ATIS and visual
  - Wind direction and speed
  - Gusts
  - Determine into-wind heading for landing
- [ ] **Approach path planning** - Brief with crew
  - Entry point to pattern
  - Pattern altitude (typically 500 ft AGL for military)
  - Final approach course
  - Obstacles (wires, towers, buildings)
  - Abort/go-around plan
- [ ] **Fuel quantity (EUFD FUEL page)** - Check
  - Verify sufficient fuel on board
  - Note for ground crew if immediate refuel needed
- [ ] **Landing brief (Pilot to CPG)** - Coordinate
  - "Standard overhead pattern, left-hand, runway 27, 500 feet, winds 270 at 10"
  - CPG acknowledges and confirms understanding

## Pattern Entry (Overhead Pattern - Military Standard)

- [ ] **Initial approach** - Enter overhead
  - Altitude: 500 ft AGL
  - Airspeed: 100-120 KIAS
  - Overfly runway/landing area centerline
  - Verify field layout, wind sock, obstacles
- [ ] **Break** - Commence at appropriate point
  - At departure end of runway, or abeam landing spot
  - 30-degree bank turn (left or right as briefed)
  - Begin deceleration: Reduce power, aft cyclic
  - Target airspeed: 80 KIAS in the turn
- [ ] **Downwind leg** - Establish
  - Altitude: 500 ft AGL
  - Airspeed: 60-80 KIAS
  - Parallel to runway/landing area
  - Spacing: ~1/2 mile offset from centerline
  - Complete landing checklist on downwind

## Before Landing Checklist (On Downwind)

- [ ] **Master arm switch** - SAFE (guard down)
- [ ] **Landing lights (overhead console)** - ON
  - Nose light - ON
  - Tail light - As required
- [ ] **Anti-collision lights** - ON (strobes)
- [ ] **Position/navigation lights** - As required (night)
- [ ] **Rotor RPM (EUFD)** - Check 101% NP
- [ ] **Engine instruments (EUFD)** - Check normal
  - NG: ~72% both engines
  - TGT: Green band both engines
  - Oil pressure: Green band
- [ ] **Hydraulic pressure (EUFD HYD page)** - Check
  - Primary: 2,850-3,250 psi
  - Utility: 2,850-3,250 psi
- [ ] **Fuel quantity** - Note remaining fuel
- [ ] **Seat harness** - Check tight (both crew)

## Base Leg

- [ ] **Turn to base** - At appropriate point
  - Abeam landing spot, or at designated point
  - 30-degree bank turn
  - Continue deceleration: 40-60 KIAS
  - Altitude: 500 ft AGL descending to 300-400 ft
- [ ] **Landing area visual** - Verify in sight
  - Keep landing spot in view during turn
  - Assess wind drift

## Final Approach

- [ ] **Turn to final** - Align with landing direction
  - Roll out on centerline/into wind
  - Airspeed: 40-60 KIAS
  - Altitude: 200-300 ft AGL
  - Descent rate: 300-500 fpm
- [ ] **Approach angle** - Establish 6-9 degree glidepath
  - Visual glidepath (VASI/PAPI if available for runway)
  - Aim for landing spot
  - Adjust collective to control descent rate
- [ ] **Airspeed control** - Monitor and maintain
  - 60 KIAS at 300 ft
  - 40-50 KIAS at 100 ft
  - 30-40 KIAS at 50 ft
  - Reduce to near-zero over landing spot
- [ ] **Altitude monitoring** - Radar altimeter primary
  - Callouts (CPG assists): "100 feet... 50 feet... 30 feet..."
- [ ] **Landing area alignment** - Verify centered
  - Pedals to align nose with landing direction
  - Cyclic to track toward landing spot (correct for wind drift)
- [ ] **Obstacles** - Verify clear
  - Wires, towers, vehicles, personnel
  - Clear final approach path
- [ ] **Wind check** - Monitor for gusts/shifts
  - Adjust power and cyclic for wind
- [ ] **Engine instruments (EUFD)** - Monitor during approach
  - Both engines stable
  - No cautions/warnings
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP
  - Critical during descent and landing
  - If NP drops, increase collective/power

## Landing (Pilot - Flight Controls)

### Final 50 Feet to Touchdown

- [ ] **Airspeed reduction** - Slow to near-zero groundspeed
  - 30-40 KIAS at 50 ft
  - 20-30 KIAS at 30 ft
  - 10-20 KIAS at 10 ft
  - 0-10 KIAS at touchdown
  - Use forward cyclic to decelerate (nose-up attitude bleeds speed)
- [ ] **Rate of descent** - Control with collective
  - 200-300 fpm typical during final approach
  - Reduce to 100-200 fpm below 50 ft
  - 50-100 fpm for final 10 ft
  - Smooth collective inputs to cushion descent
- [ ] **Ground effect** - Anticipate at 35-50 ft AGL
  - Aircraft becomes more efficient in ground effect
  - Less power required to maintain altitude
  - Descent rate may decrease - adjust collective down slightly
- [ ] **Collective management** - Fine adjustments
  - Increase collective = reduce descent rate
  - Decrease collective = increase descent rate
  - Small, smooth inputs near ground
- [ ] **Cyclic control** - Maintain level attitude
  - Slight nose-up attitude during final deceleration
  - Level aircraft just before touchdown
  - Adjust for wind drift (into wind = forward cyclic)
- [ ] **Pedals (anti-torque)** - Maintain heading
  - Keep nose aligned with landing direction (into wind ideal)
  - Prevent yaw oscillations
  - Small corrections only
- [ ] **Visual scan** - Pilot eyes outside
  - Look at landing spot and horizon (not instruments)
  - Peripheral vision for drift and attitude
  - CPG provides altitude callouts ("30 feet... 20... 10...")

### Touchdown

- [ ] **Touchdown point** - Aim for center of landing area
- [ ] **Touchdown attitude** - Nearly level
  - Slight nose-up acceptable (3-5 degrees)
  - Avoid tail-low (tail strike risk)
  - Avoid nose-low (can cause rollover)
- [ ] **Touchdown sequence** - Simultaneous if possible
  - Ideal: All three wheels touch together (main gear + tailwheel)
  - Acceptable: Main gear slightly first, then tailwheel
  - Avoid: Tailwheel first (unstable)
- [ ] **Touchdown firmness** - Gentle but positive
  - Not hovering inches above ground (wastes fuel)
  - Not slamming down (hard on gear)
  - Firm enough to clearly be on ground
- [ ] **Collective after touchdown** - Lower smoothly
  - Once on ground, gradually lower collective
  - Full down within 2-3 seconds
  - Keeps aircraft planted (reduces bounce)
- [ ] **Cyclic after touchdown** - Center and hold
  - Slight forward pressure to prevent tailwheel-first rollover
  - Keep aircraft stable on gear
  - Prevent drift
- [ ] **Pedals after touchdown** - Maintain heading
  - Keep nose pointed in landing direction
  - Prevent ground resonance (rare but possible)

## After Landing (On Ground)

- [ ] **Collective** - Full down and detent locked
  - Push collective fully down
  - Engage friction lock
- [ ] **Flight controls** - Neutralize
  - Cyclic centered
  - Pedals centered
- [ ] **Parking brake (if available)** - Set
  - Prevents aircraft roll on slope
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP
  - Keep engines at flight idle until ready to shut down
  - Or if taxiing, maintain flight idle
- [ ] **Wheel chocks** - Request/verify
  - Ground crew places chocks
  - Verify chocked before engine shutdown (if windy or slope)
- [ ] **Landing lights** - OFF (unless taxiing at night)
- [ ] **After landing checklist** - Complete if taxiing or if extended ground operations

## Hot Refuel (FARP - Engines Running)

### Setup

- [ ] **Position aircraft** - Centered in refuel point
- [ ] **Parking brake** - Set (or chocks placed)
- [ ] **Collective** - Full down and locked
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP (flight idle)
- [ ] **Master arm switch (Right console)** - SAFE (guard down)
- [ ] **Weapons** - All safed
- [ ] **APU** - OFF (not needed, engines provide power)
- [ ] **Rotor disc clearance** - Verify ground crew aware
  - No personnel within rotor arc
  - Approach from 10 and 2 o'clock positions only

### Ground Crew Coordination

- [ ] **Crew chief/refueler** - Visual contact
  - Pilot signals ready for refuel
  - Ground crew signals ready to connect
- [ ] **Static discharge** - Ground crew grounds aircraft
  - Grounding cable connected before fuel cap opened
  - Prevents static spark/fire
- [ ] **No smoking enforced** - Verify 50 ft radius
- [ ] **Fire extinguisher** - Verify ground crew has extinguisher ready

### During Refuel

- [ ] **Engine instruments (EUFD)** - Monitor
  - Both engines stable at flight idle
  - No cautions/warnings
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP
- [ ] **Fuel quantity (EUFD FUEL page)** - Monitor increase
  - Watch fuel gauge rise
  - Note when approaching full (~1,420 lbs internal)
- [ ] **Crew alertness** - Both crew monitor
  - CPG watches refuel operation
  - Pilot monitors instruments and area
- [ ] **Communication with ground crew** - Continuous
  - ICS to ground crew (if equipped) or hand signals
  - Stop fueling when full or at desired quantity

### Hot Refuel Complete

- [ ] **Fuel cap** - Verify closed and secured by ground crew
- [ ] **Grounding cable** - Disconnected
- [ ] **Ground crew clear** - Visual confirmation
  - All personnel clear of rotor arc
  - Thumbs up from crew chief
- [ ] **Fuel quantity** - Final check on EUFD
- [ ] **Ready for departure** - If continuing mission

## Hot Rearm (FARP - Engines Running)

### Setup

- [ ] **Position aircraft** - In rearm area
- [ ] **Parking brake** - Set
- [ ] **Collective** - Full down and locked
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP
- [ ] **Master arm switch** - SAFE (guard down)
- [ ] **WPN page** - Display to monitor weapons status
- [ ] **All weapons** - SAFE
  - Gun - SAFE
  - Missiles - SAFE
  - Laser - OFF

### Armament Crew Coordination

- [ ] **Armament crew** - Visual contact
  - Crew chief briefs what weapons will be loaded
  - Pilot acknowledges
- [ ] **Rotor disc clearance** - Ensure crew aware
  - Approach from approved angles only
  - No one enters rotor arc
- [ ] **Pylon access** - Armament crew accesses weapons pylons
  - Hellfire rails
  - Rocket pods
  - Gun ammo bay (if rearming gun)

### During Rearm

- [ ] **Weapon loading** - Observe via mirrors or CPG
  - Hellfires loaded on rails
  - Rockets loaded in pods
  - Gun ammo belt loaded (requires access panel)
- [ ] **WPN page** - Monitor for changes
  - Weapon count should update as missiles/rockets loaded
  - Verify no faults appear
- [ ] **Safety pins** - Armament crew installs during loading
  - Keeps weapons safe during installation
  - Must be removed before flight

### Hot Rearm Complete

- [ ] **Armament crew** - Signals complete
  - All weapons loaded
  - All access panels closed and secured
- [ ] **Safety pins removed** - Verify
  - Armament crew shows removed pins to pilot
  - Pins stowed (not left on aircraft)
- [ ] **WPN page verification** - Check weapons inventory
  - Hellfire count correct
  - Rocket count correct
  - Gun rounds correct (if rearmed)
  - All weapons show READY status (no red X)
- [ ] **Ground crew clear** - Visual confirmation
  - All personnel clear
  - Thumbs up from crew chief
- [ ] **Ready for departure** - If continuing mission

## Shutdown After Landing (Normal)

### Before Shutdown

- [ ] **Position** - Parked in designated area
- [ ] **Parking brake** - Set
- [ ] **Collective** - Full down and locked
- [ ] **Wheel chocks** - Placed by ground crew

### Systems Shutdown Sequence

- [ ] **Master arm switch** - SAFE (guard down)
- [ ] **WPN page** - Verify all weapons safed
- [ ] **FCR** - OFF
  - Press FCR power knob to OFF
- [ ] **TADS** - Stow
  - Slew TADS to stow position (nose down)
  - TADS power - OFF
- [ ] **ASE** - OFF
  - RWR - OFF
  - CMWS - OFF
  - LWS - OFF
- [ ] **MPDs (CPG)** - OFF
  - Left and Right MPD power switches - OFF
- [ ] **TEDAC** - OFF
- [ ] **Radios** - Set as required (or OFF if completely shutting down)
- [ ] **Transponder/IFF** - STBY or OFF

## Engine Shutdown

### Shutdown Sequence

- [ ] **Collective** - Verify full down and detent locked
- [ ] **Cyclic and pedals** - Centered
- [ ] **Engine 1 PCL (Power Control Lever)** - OFF
  - Pull PCL aft to OFF position
  - ENG 1 STOP light illuminates briefly
  - Monitor EUFD Engine page:
    - NG (Gas Producer) begins decreasing
    - NP (Power Turbine) begins decreasing
    - TGT drops
  - Wait for Engine 1 NG below 10%
- [ ] **Engine 1 shutdown complete** - Verify
  - NG: 0%
  - NP: Still turning (driven by Engine 2 via rotor system)
  - TGT: Dropping
- [ ] **Engine 2 PCL** - OFF (after Engine 1 NG below 10%)
  - Pull PCL aft to OFF position
  - ENG 2 STOP light illuminates
  - Monitor EUFD:
    - NG decreasing
    - NP decreasing (rotor system spooling down)
    - TGT dropping
- [ ] **Rotor RPM decay** - Monitor
  - NP decreases from 101% toward zero
  - Decay time: ~2-3 minutes for full rotor stop
- [ ] **Rotor brake** - Engage when NP below 40%
  - Press rotor brake switch (overhead console)
  - Rotor deceleration increases
  - Rotor stops within 30-60 seconds
  - Do NOT engage brake above 40% NP (can damage brake)
- [ ] **Both engines shutdown** - Verify EUFD
  - NG: 0% both engines
  - NP: 0%
  - TGT: Cooling down

### Post-Shutdown

- [ ] **APU start (if required)** - For ground power or cooling
  - Press APU button
  - APU spools up to 100%
  - APU GEN comes online
  - Provides electrical power without battery drain
- [ ] **Pilot MPDs** - OFF
  - Left and Right MPD switches - OFF
- [ ] **Avionics** - OFF or as required for maintenance
- [ ] **Collective** - Verify full down and locked
- [ ] **Cyclic** - Push full forward and secure (tie-down if long-term)
- [ ] **Pedals** - Centered
- [ ] **Lights** - All OFF
  - Landing lights - OFF
  - Anti-collision - OFF
  - Position - OFF
- [ ] **Master ignition switch** - OFF (both engines)
- [ ] **Battery switch** - OFF (after APU stable or external power connected)
  - Verify APU still running (if needed) before battery off
  - If no APU/external power, EUFD will go dark when battery off

## Securing Aircraft (Long-Term Parking)

- [ ] **Main rotor blades** - Tie-downs installed
  - Secure blades to prevent wind damage
- [ ] **Tail rotor** - Secure
- [ ] **Engine covers** - Install as required
  - Protect from FOD, weather, birds
- [ ] **Pitot covers** - Install
- [ ] **TADS/PNVS covers** - Install to protect sensors
- [ ] **Wheel chocks** - Verify in place (front and rear of wheels)
- [ ] **Canopy/doors** - Closed and secured
- [ ] **Ground locks** - Install if available (landing gear, flight controls)
- [ ] **Maintenance forms/logbook** - Complete
  - Flight hours
  - Fuel remaining
  - Weapons status
  - Discrepancies (if any)
  - Crew signatures

## Autorotation (Emergency - Engine Failure)

### Immediate Actions

- [ ] **Autorotation entry** - IMMEDIATE when engine(s) fail
  - Lower collective immediately (full down quickly)
  - Maintains rotor RPM as aircraft descends
  - Without collective down, rotor RPM decays rapidly = loss of control
- [ ] **Airspeed** - Establish 60-80 KIAS
  - Forward cyclic to accelerate if too slow
  - Aft cyclic to decelerate if too fast
  - 60-80 KIAS = best glide speed for AH-64
- [ ] **Rotor RPM** - Maintain 101% NP (green band)
  - Critical - must maintain rotor energy
  - Adjust collective slightly if NP drifts
  - If NP low = raise collective slightly
  - If NP high = lower collective more

### Mayday Call (Time Permitting)

- [ ] **Radio call** - Transmit
  - "MAYDAY MAYDAY MAYDAY, Apache 123, engine failure, autorotating, position [location]"
  - Squawk 7700 on transponder

### Landing Site Selection

- [ ] **Best available landing area** - Scan and select
  - Open field, road, clearing
  - Avoid wires, trees, water if possible
  - Wind direction (into wind ideal but not critical)
  - Size: Larger = safer

### Autorotation Descent

- [ ] **Glide to landing site** - Manage descent
  - Airspeed: 60-80 KIAS
  - Rotor RPM: 101% NP (green)
  - Plan approach to reach selected site
  - Adjust flight path with cyclic
- [ ] **Collective management** - Conserve rotor energy
  - Keep collective full down during descent
  - Only raise for final flare and touchdown

### Flare and Touchdown (Critical Phase)

- [ ] **Flare** - Initiate at 50-100 ft AGL
  - Aft cyclic (nose up)
  - Bleeds off forward airspeed
  - Converts forward speed into rotor energy (NP increases)
  - Reduces rate of descent
- [ ] **Level** - Reduce aft cyclic as airspeed approaches zero
  - Cyclic forward to level aircraft before touchdown
  - Aircraft should be nearly level at touchdown
- [ ] **Collective** - Apply at touchdown
  - At 5-10 ft AGL and nearly zero airspeed: Pull collective up
  - Uses stored rotor energy to cushion landing
  - Touchdown should be firm but survivable
- [ ] **Post-touchdown** - Secure aircraft
  - Collective full down
  - PCLs verify OFF
  - Rotor brake - Do NOT apply (rotor still spinning)
  - Assess crew and aircraft condition

### Emergency Egress (If Required)

- [ ] **Rotor stop** - Wait for rotor to stop (or slow significantly)
  - Do not exit while rotor spinning at high speed
- [ ] **Crew egress** - Exit aircraft
  - CPG exits front
  - Pilot exits rear
  - Move away from aircraft (50+ feet)
- [ ] **ELT (Emergency Locator Transmitter)** - Activate if remote area
- [ ] **First aid** - Attend to injuries
- [ ] **Survival** - Assess situation, water, shelter, communication

## Precautionary Landing (Aircraft Malfunction, Not Failure)

- [ ] **Problem identification** - Analyze malfunction
  - Caution/Warning panel indications
  - Engine instruments
  - Flight control degradation
  - Hydraulic issues
  - Electrical problems
- [ ] **Landing decision** - When to execute
  - Continued flight unsafe
  - Situation may worsen
  - Better to land now than risk crash later
- [ ] **Landing site selection** - Choose best available
  - Airport/helipad ideal
  - Open field acceptable
  - Assess wind, obstacles, size
- [ ] **Crew coordination** - Brief situation
  - Pilot and CPG discuss problem and plan
  - Both crew ready for abnormal landing
- [ ] **Approach** - Conservative profile
  - Higher/slower approach than normal
  - Shallower descent rate
  - Prepared for degraded control response
- [ ] **Systems monitoring** - Watch affected systems
  - If hydraulics degraded: Heavier control forces
  - If engine failing: Monitor NG/TGT/NP closely
  - If electrical failing: Note what instruments still working
- [ ] **Normal landing technique** - Execute with caution
  - Land as normally as possible given degraded systems
  - Touchdown may be firmer than normal
- [ ] **Immediate shutdown** - Consider based on situation
  - Fire: Shutdown immediately, evacuate
  - Severe damage: Shutdown and assess
  - Minor issue: Can taxi to parking
- [ ] **Emergency services** - Request if needed
  - Fire trucks, medics, crash recovery
  - Report nature of malfunction
